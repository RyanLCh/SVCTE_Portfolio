College and Career Plans

Ryan La

Career Goal #1

My main hobby and interest is in computer programming, so I’m aiming for a career related to software engineering or web development. I perform better in math-related subjects, so it fits within my scope of knowledge. Software engineers use programming to build software applications and computer programs, and web developers design and code a website and its network and security.

I would like to major in Computer Science and Programming at Stanford University, University of California Berkeley, or California Polytechnical State University. Stanford University is the most desirable because it is somewhat close to home, and a lot of tech companies are stationed in the bay area. Additionally, it is well renowned for its computer engineering, especially as it has an artificial intelligence program. Ultimately, I will major in something in computer science and mathematics.

Career Goal #2

If that doesn’t work out, I could focus more on getting work experience rather than focusing solely on academics. While in college, I would work for multiple jobs, and after graduating, I would use that experience to gain the upper hand in applying for jobs. Then, I’d jump from company to company to expand my skills and end at a workplace I like.

To do so, I would probably go to a community college to save money and for a lighter workload as I first start building my job skills. Then, I’d try to transfer to a university, where I would develop my computer science skills. I’ve saved enough money for college, and my other jobs should be able to pay for my other living expenses and books.
